Senior Portraits, Done Differently

Not School Pictures. Not Stiff Posing.

Senior portraits should feel personal, not generic — and a lot of seniors feel genuinely awkward in front of a camera, no matter how confident they are everywhere else. Parents want images that feel polished, but still true to who their senior actually is.

We guide the entire session from start to finish, so no one is left guessing what to do with their hands or how to stand. The result is portraits that feel confident and editorial, while still looking unmistakably like your senior — not a template, and not a performance.

We work with senior families throughout Austin and the surrounding area, including Round Rock, Georgetown, Cedar Park, Hutto, Pflugerville, Leander, Westlake, and Bee Cave.

Why It Matters

They Will Only Be This Version of Themselves Once

Senior year is a transition — the last stretch of being exactly this age, in exactly this place, before everything starts to shift. These portraits aren't just for graduation announcements. They become family keepsakes, the photographs you'll pull out years from now to remember who your senior was right at this moment.

That's the goal behind every session: not just a flattering photo, but a real, lasting record of who your senior is right now.

Questions

Senior Portrait FAQ

When should we book senior portraits?
Most families book in spring or summer of junior year through early senior year, which gives the most flexibility on dates and locations. Earlier is generally easier, but we work with families on whatever timeline fits.
Do you photograph Class of 2027 seniors?
Yes. We're currently scheduling senior sessions for the Class of 2027 throughout Austin and the surrounding area.
Do you help with posing?
Yes. We guide posture, movement, and expression throughout the session, so your senior is never left wondering what to do with their hands or how to stand.
What should my senior wear?
We'll send a complimentary wardrobe guide before the session, but generally a mix of a few outfits that feel like your senior's everyday style works best.
Can we include sports, dance, music, or hobbies?
Yes, and we encourage it. A senior's interests — sports, dance, music, art, anything that's genuinely theirs — make for some of the most personal portraits in the gallery.
Can parents help choose images?
Of course. Parents are welcome at the session and in the gallery review process, and we're happy to talk through favorites together.
How long does a senior session take?
Most sessions run one to two hours depending on the collection and the number of locations or looks involved.
When do we receive the gallery?
Senior galleries are typically delivered within two to three weeks, with a few favorites shared sooner.
Do you offer prints?
Yes. Prints, albums, and wall art are available, and personal printing rights are included with every collection.
What is NP Seniors?
NP Seniors is a separate, limited program for the Class of 2027 — built for seniors who want a more connected experience with seasonal sessions, featured portraits, and early access.
