Wedding Photography at Mattie's: A Planning Guide

South Austin, Texas

A historic farmhouse and restaurant property known for its cypress-lined lawn and resident peacocks.

About the Venue

A Farmhouse With Decades of History

Mattie's sits on a property that began as a 1916 farmhouse in South Austin. By the 1940s, it had become a restaurant known as Green Pastures, and in more recent years it has operated under the name Mattie's. That layered history shows up in the property's character: a wraparound-porch farmhouse, a lawn shaded by towering cypress trees, and grounds well-worn by decades of Austin gatherings.

One detail couples often mention is the venue's resident peacocks, which roam the grounds and occasionally make an appearance during events — a small, distinctive touch that's become part of Mattie's identity.

Why Couples Choose It

Southern Charm With Established History

Mattie's tends to appeal to couples drawn to a garden-party feel with genuine historic roots, rather than a newly built venue designed to look historic. The combination of a farmhouse interior, mature trees, and an open lawn gives it a softer, more pastoral character than many of Austin's more polished event spaces.

We haven't photographed here ourselves yet, but based on the layout and the light a cypress-shaded lawn typically gives, we'd expect this to be a strong location for relaxed, dappled-light portraits — the kind that don't need much beyond what the trees are already doing.

Photography Considerations

What to Think About for Photos

Natural Light

An open lawn shaded by mature cypress trees suggests strong, dappled natural light for outdoor portraits — worth confirming with the venue how that light shifts across the day.

Indoor Spaces

The farmhouse interior, with its wraparound porch and historic windows, likely offers warm, character-filled light for indoor portraits and getting-ready photos.

Outdoor Spaces

The lawn and gardens appear to be the heart of the property — a strong candidate for both ceremony and portrait locations, weather permitting.

Weather Considerations

As a primarily outdoor-oriented property, it's worth asking Mattie's directly what their rain plan looks like for both ceremony and reception.

Quick Answers

Common Planning Questions

We haven't photographed at Mattie's yet, so these answers are based on the venue's publicly described layout rather than firsthand experience — worth confirming directly with the venue.

Is Mattie's better for daytime or evening weddings?

Likely daytime, given the emphasis on the lawn and cypress trees — that natural light is probably the venue's strongest asset.

Where are the best portrait locations?

Based on the description, the cypress-lined lawn and the farmhouse's wraparound porch both seem like strong candidates.

Is a first look recommended here?

Possibly, if maximizing lawn daylight matters to you — worth asking the venue about their preferred ceremony timing.

What's the rain backup?

Unclear from public information — this is a key question to ask the venue directly, since the property reads as primarily outdoor-oriented.

What's the best season to get married here?

Texas spring or fall, most likely, to make the most of the lawn without summer heat.

Can family photos happen indoors?

The farmhouse interior may offer space, but worth confirming room size and availability with the venue.

Is flash allowed?

We don't have direct experience here — always confirm flash and lighting policies with the venue directly before your wedding.

Planning Tips

Questions to Ask the Venue

•

Ask what their weather backup plan looks like for an outdoor ceremony on the lawn.

•

Ask whether the peacocks are reliably present, in case you're hoping for that detail in your photos.

•

Confirm current guest capacity and whether the farmhouse interior is available for getting-ready photos.

•

Ask about restrictions on timing, since the property has operated as a working restaurant historically.
