About the Venue

A Downtown Hotel, Set on the Water

The Four Seasons Hotel Austin sits directly on Lady Bird Lake in downtown Austin, giving it a setting that few other hotel venues in the city can match. An outdoor lawn ceremony space is framed by heritage oak trees, while the hotel's ballroom — complete with chandeliers — handles the reception indoors.

The combination of downtown convenience and genuine waterfront setting is what sets this property apart: guests get skyline views from one direction and lake views from the other, all within the same address.

Lakefront Hotel Heritage Oaks Downtown Austin Full-Service Venue
Why Couples Choose It

Skyline and Lake, in One Setting

Couples choose the Four Seasons Austin when they want the convenience of a downtown hotel without sacrificing a genuine outdoor ceremony setting. The heritage oaks on the lawn give the ceremony space a softness that pure hotel venues often lack, while the ballroom delivers the polish of a five-star property for the reception.

Shooting Alexandra and Matt's wedding here, what stood out to us was how much the lake adds to portraits — the water gives a sense of openness that's hard to find this close to downtown.

Photography Considerations

Light, Layout, and Timing

Natural Light

The heritage oaks on the lawn provide natural shade and texture, while the open lake view offers strong, unobstructed light.

Indoor Spaces

The ballroom's chandeliers and architectural details give it elegance that photographs well without much added lighting.

Golden Hour

The lakefront lawn is an exceptional golden hour location, with the water reflecting the changing light.

Reception Lighting

The ballroom's existing chandeliers create a warm, formal atmosphere well-suited to evening receptions.

Quick Answers

Common Planning Questions

Is this venue better for daytime or evening weddings?

Both work well, but evening ceremonies make the most of golden hour light over the lake.

Where are the best portrait locations?

The lakefront lawn under the heritage oaks was the standout location shooting Alexandra and Matt's wedding.

Is a first look recommended here?

It can work well on the lawn, giving you quiet time by the water before guests arrive.

What's the rain backup?

The hotel's indoor ballroom serves as a reliable backup for both ceremony and reception if needed.

Can family photos happen indoors?

Yes — the ballroom offers ample space for group formals before or after the reception.

Is flash allowed?

We didn't encounter restrictions photographing Alexandra and Matt's wedding, but always confirm current venue policy when you book.

Planning Tips

What We'd Suggest

•

If lakefront portraits matter to you, plan your timeline around golden hour on the lawn.

•

Ask the hotel about room block rates early if you have many out-of-town guests.

•

Confirm the lawn's weather backup plan in case of rain on your ceremony date.

•

Build in travel time if your ceremony is elsewhere, as it was for Alexandra and Matt's day from St. Mary Cathedral.
